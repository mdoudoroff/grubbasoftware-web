Purchase

Due to the collapse of our payment processor, Kagi, TrueGrain is not currently available for purchase. Sorry for the inconvenience.

Individual TrueGrain licenses are available for purchase on-line through a Web browser or from within a menu item in the demo software itself. The cost is US$49.95. Payment is handled by Kagi.com through credit card and whatever other payment methods Kagi supports for your location.

Once Kagi processes your payment, you are issued a registration code that can be used to convert TrueGrain from demo mode into a fully functional edition. If you buy your license through the Web, that registration code is delivered by email and must be manually copied and pasted into a registration form in the software. If you buy your license from the menu item within TrueGrain, the registration code is installed automatically.

When you purchase a TrueGrain 1.x license:

  • You are entitled to download and install any point-release upgrades we issue at no extra charge. (If we produce a 2.0 version, there may be an upgrade fee.)
  • You are entitled to download and install any film packs we issue for TrueGrain 1.x at no extra charge. (Note: film packs issued for future versions of TrueGrain may not be “backwards-compatible” with older versions of TrueGrain.)

Unlocking the software

If you have not downloaded and installed TrueGrain, please do so. If you receive an activation code by email, launch TrueGrain and choose Software Registration from the Help menu. Paste in the email address you used to register the software, along with the registration code we supply you with, and you should be done.

TrueGrain for Macintosh

TrueGrain for Macintosh requires a Mac with at least 2GB RAM and OS X 10.6 (Snow Leopard) or later.

TrueGrain for Windows

TrueGrain requires a PC with at least 2GB RAM and Windows XP with Service Pack 2 or later.
